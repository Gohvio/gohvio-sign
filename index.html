How Snail Sign protects your documents

Plain answers, no legal jargon.

Your document stays on your computer until you send it. Preparing a document happens entirely in your browser. Nothing is uploaded anywhere until you click Send.
Signing links can't be guessed or forged. When you send, the document goes into private, access-controlled storage. Your signer receives a unique, cryptographically signed link. Changing it, pointing it at another document, or extending its life simply breaks it.
Links expire after 14 days. If a signing link is ever shared or leaks later, it stops working on its own.
Documents delete themselves. The moment a document is fully signed, the stored copy is automatically deleted. Documents that never get signed are cleaned up after the link expires. We don't keep an archive of your files.
Every signed PDF includes a certificate of completion. The last page records who signed, when they signed, and a SHA-256 digital fingerprint of the signed document. If even one character changes afterwards, the fingerprint no longer matches, so tampering is provable.
Nobody else is copied. Emails go only to the signers and the sender. Signing pages are served with no-cache and no-referrer headers, so browsers and networks don't keep copies along the way.
What we never do. We don't read your documents, sell your data, or use your files to train AI. Documents exist in our storage only long enough to collect signatures.
